Bhallaladeva - Baahubali Franchise

Bhallaladeva's Story

Bhallaladeva (pronounced: BHAAL-laal-DAYV) is a main character and recurring antagonist in the Baahubali Franchise, and the main antagonist of the movies. He is Lord Bijjaladeva's and Rajmatha Sivagami's son, cousin of Amarendra Baahubali, and uncle of Mahendra Baahubali. He is a traitorous prince and jealous of his adoptive brother's popularity and has plotted against killing him and taking Mahishmati for himself, influenced by his father since childhood. He has conspired with Pradhan Guru to disgrace Amarendra time and again in Baahubali: The Lost Legends and orders Baahubali's execution in Baahubali: The Conclusion. He is killed by Devasena in Baahubali: The Conclusion.

Bhallaladeva wants to create a wedge between Sivagami and Amarendra by requesting for marriage with Devasena. Sivagami agrees not knowing Amarendra is in love with Devasena. The misunderstanding comes to light in Mahismati and Amarendra has to choose the throne or Devasena, he chooses Devasena causing Bhallaladeva to be king and Amarendra to be commander in chief, though the people show their preference to Amarendra.

At Devasena's baby shower, Bhallaladeva taunts Devasena causing to yell at him and a further assault gets Devasena and Amarendra banished from the palace. Despite being banished, they are still living happily outside, annoying Bhallaladeva. Bhallaladeva and Bijjaladeva hatch a plan and Sivagami orders Kattappa to kill Amarendra. Kattappa kills Amarendra and learns he is innocent. Sivagami tells the kingdom the news and shows the baby, Mahendra Baahubali, who she escapes with. She is killed by Bhallaladeva, who imprisons Devasena. Bhallaladeva destroys Kunthala under his tyranny.

Shivudu plans to avenge his father's death by raising an army of his own with help from Kattappa. They invade Mahishmati and destroy the statue during the war and Bhallaladeva is burnt alive on a pyre by Devasena.

Superhuman Strength

Bhallaladeva possesses a tremendous amount of strength through a combination of training and genetics. He can easily take on multiple opponents at the same time and has been capable of lifting and breaking objects that would normally require dozens to hundreds of men. His strength was only matched by Amarendra and Mahendra Bahubali and it was surpassed by Dharamraj and Pradhan Guru.
